THE RE-INTRODUCTION OF OTTERS (LUTRA LUTRA) IN THE CZECH REPUBLIC 
 
Aleš TOMAN1, Marcela ROCHE2, Kevin ROCHE2 
 
1 Agency of Nature & Landscape Protection, Pavlov 54, 504 01, Ledec nad Sazavou, Czech Republic  email:  
pavlov.nature@worldonline.cz 
2 Czech Otter Foundation Fund, P.O. Box 53, Trebon 379 01, Czech Republic  email: otter@box.cz   
 
 
As in the rest of Europe, otter (Lutra lutra) distribution declined dramatically in the Czech 
Republic. This decline resulted in three isolated populations; small areas in the north–west 
and east, and the strongest, a true meta-population, in the southern part of the Czech Republic, 
based around the carp fishpond region. To prevent a potential genetic bottleneck and 
instability of the otter population a reintroduction programme was initiated in 1990. The 
program comprised three phases: an investigation phase (1994-1997), an experimental 
reintroduction (1997), and reintroduction (1998 – 2003). During the first phase, potential 
areas were selected and investigated as regards fish population, bank vegetation, pollution and 
potential bottlenecks. In the second stage, three males and one female were experimentally 
released at one of the most suitable sites. Telemetry monitoring of the animals confirmed a 
good integration in the area. During the main phase of reintroduction, 25 individuals were 
released into four river catchments (Moravice, Morava, Orlice and Odra). To date, a total of 
29 animals have been introduced (11 females and 18 males), 9 of which were born in 
captivity and 20 found in nature as abandoned or injured cubs. Reproduction has been 
confirmed and one female was found as a road kill 8 months after being released.
