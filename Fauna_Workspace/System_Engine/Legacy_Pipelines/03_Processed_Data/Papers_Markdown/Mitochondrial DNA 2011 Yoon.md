MITOGENOME ANNOUNCEMENT
The complete mitochondrial genome of the greater horseshoe bat
subspecies, Rhinolophus ferrumequinum korai (Chiroptera:
Rhinolophidae)
KWANG BAE YOON1, JI YOUNG KIM1, JAE YOUL CHO2, & YUNG CHUL PARK1
1Department of Forest Environment Protection, College of Forest and Environmental Sciences, Kangwon National University,

Abstract
The total length of the mitogenome of Rhinolophus ferrumequinum korai is 16,839 bp with a total base composition of 31.8% A,
25.4% T, 28.7% C, and 14.0% G. The mitogenome consists of 13 protein-coding genes, 2 rRNA (12S and 16S RNA) genes,
22 tRNA genes, and 1 control region.
Keywords: Greater horseshoe bat, Rhinolophus ferrumequinum korai, mitogenome
Cave bats of Rhinolophus ferrumequinum, often called
the greater horseshoe bat, have a wide range in the
Palaearctic, occurring in North Africa, southern
Europe, and southern Asia (Rossiter et al. 2000;
Aulagnier et al. 2008). There are two subspecies of R.
ferrumequinum currently recognized in South Korea.
Rhinolophus ferrumequinum korai is distributed in
mainlandofSouthKorea,whereasRhinolophusferrume-
quinum quelpartis inhabits the Jeju Island, which is
located in the most southern part of the country.
In the present paper, we announce characteristics of
the complete mitochondrial genome (JN392460) of a
Korean greater horseshoe bat subspecies, R. f. korai.
Tissues for total genomic DNA extraction were
collected from patagium of a greater horseshoe bat.
A
previously
published
mitochondrial
genome
(NC005433) of the formosan lesser horseshoe
bat (Rhinolophus monoceros; Lin et al. 2002) was used
to design PCR ampliﬁcation primers (22 pairs) and
as a template for gene annotation of R. f. korai
mitogenome.
Overall, characteristics including the mitochondrial
genome organization and the gene arrangement
pattern were similar
to the typical vertebrate
mitochondrial genomes. The total length of the
mitogenome of R. f. korai is 16,839 bp with a total
base composition of 31.8% A, 25.4% T, 28.7% C, and
14.0% G. The mitogenome consists of 13 protein-
coding genes, 2 rRNA (12S and 16S RNA) genes, 22
tRNA genes, and 1 control region (CR; Table I).
Except for the eight tRNA genes and ND6, all the
other mitochondrial genes are encoded on the heavy
strand. The total length of the protein-coding gene
sequences is 11,408 bp. Except for ND2, ND3, and
ND5 (ATA start codon), the remaining protein-coding
genes initiate with ATG. Eight protein-coding genes

21 tRNA genes could be folded into cloverleaf
secondary structure. The H-strand replication origin
(OL) is located between tRNAAsn gene and tRNACys
gene within the WANCY region containing ﬁve
tRNA genes (tRNATrp, tRNAAla, tRNAAsn, tRNACys,

rRNA gene was 2532 bp and the tRNAVal intervenes
between the two rRNA genes.
The CR or
D-loop region is located between
tRNAPro and tRNAPhe and is 1291 bp in length.
When the genomes of R. f. korai and of R. monoceros
were
compared,
the
aligned
sequences
were
16,968 bp long, with the inclusion of gaps (129
sites). The total nucleotide similarity of the two
genomes was 88.0% varying from 78.8% to 100%
along the gene regions. The present study will
contribute to understanding taxonomic status and
genetic divergence of Korean R. ferrumequinum
subspecies.
