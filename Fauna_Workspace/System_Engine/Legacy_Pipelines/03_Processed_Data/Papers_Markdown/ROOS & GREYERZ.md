1   INTRODUCTION  
 
Otters (Lutra lutra) in Sweden decreased dramatically in numbers and range during the 
1960s-1980s, despite the fact that hunting was banned in 1968.  In the 1980s, otters were 
found only in scattered areas in northern, east central and southern Sweden (OLSSON AND 
SANDEGREN, 1983; OLSSON, ROSENDAL AND SANDEGREN, 1984; OLSSON, SANDEGREN AND 
SJÖÅSEN, 1988).  The species had disappeared from large areas of the country, including the 
archipelagos and along the coasts.  Many reasons for the decrease of the Eurasian otter 
population have been discussed, but one in particular was pollutants, especially PCBs 
(SANDEGREN, OLSSON AND REUTERGÅRDH, 1980; MASON AND MACDONALD, 1986; OLSSON 
AND SANDEGREN, 1991).  This conclusion was partly based on results from experimental 
studies on another mustelid species, the American mink (Mustela vison), which is very 
sensitive to PCBs, but not to ∑DDT (JENSEN ET AL., 1977).  AULERICH AND RINGER (1977) 
found severe reproductive failure in mink given a mixture of PCBs.  JENSEN ET AL. (1977) set 
a limit of 50 ppm PCB (lipid weight) in mink muscle for reproductive failure.  However, in a 
recent long term study on mink, Brunström and co-workers found reproductive impairment in 
mink at levels as low as 12 µg g- 1 PCB lipid weight (BRUNSTRÖM, ET AL, 2001), indicating a 
higher sensitivity than earlier studies had claimed. 
          PCBs were banned in Sweden at the beginning of the 1970s, and since then the 
concentrations in Swedish biota have decreased.  Since the 1990s the otter population has 
increased in number and areas.    
          Concentrations of PCB in Swedish otters were very high in the past, especially in 
southern and central Sweden, but individuals from the north have also had very high 
concentrations (ROOS ET AL., 2001).  
 
 
2   MATERIAL AND METHODS 
 
Muscle samples from 49 otters collected in southern Sweden between 1970 and 2002 were 
analysed for a number of organochlorine substances.  In this short paper, data are presented on 
total PCB concentrations. All the otters were traumatically killed (i.e. killed in traffic 
accidents or drowned in fishing gear).  
          The analytical method used up to 1988 was gas chromatography using packed columns.  
After that year, GC equipped with a capillary column was used.  Ten otters were analysed 
with both methods in order to compare results.  A correction factor was established from the 
relation between the sum of the three largest peaks obtained from the capillary column (CB-
138, CB-153 and CB-180) and the sum of PCBs obtained from the packed column.  The 
correlation factor was calculated as 2.2, i.e. the sum of these three peaks x 2.2 corresponds to 
older analytical data using packed column GC.  The data are presented in ppm lipid weight.

3   RESULTS AND DISCUSSION 
 
The concentrations of total PCB ranged from 0.73 ppm to 237 ppm lipid weight.  During the 
23 year period concentrations decreased (Kendall´s τ of rank correlation: P=0.000).  The 
concentrations were highest in the 1970s.  However, in the 1990s the load seems to have 
levelled out.  Thirty-one of the otters were collected between 1990 and 2002, and 18 of them 
had concentrations above 12 ppm (15-149 ppm). (Figure 1).

For example, age and sex have not been considered in this study.  However, earlier studies

• BRUNSTRÖM, OLSSON AND ROOS (2001) also studied EROD-inducing potencies of extracts 
of livers from PCB treated mink as well as from wild Swedish otters.  Five otters with low 
body burden of total PCBs (3-8 ppm lipid weight in muscle) and five with high 
concentrations (57-300 ppm lipid weight) were compared with mink from the PCB 
experiments.  Liver extracts from the five otters with high total PCB concentrations were 
more potent than liver extracts from mink exposed to a PCB dose that caused complete

4   SUMMARY – OTTERS IN SWEDEN TODAY 
 
• The otter population in Sweden is increasing.  Several surveys as well as an increasing 
number of dead otters found indicate this. 
 
• Concentrations of PCBs in otters have decreased over time, but most clearly between the 
1970s and 1980s.  This is true for many other biota in Sweden (seals, guillemot eggs, and 
several species of fish) [see for example BIGNERT (2001a & b)].  
 
• Still large variations in concentrations of PCBs are found, and the concentrations have not 
decreased during the last decade.  More than half of the 31 otters collected between 1990 
and 2002 (58%) have concentrations above 12 ppm lipid weight.
