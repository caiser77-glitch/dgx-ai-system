1   INTRODUCTION 
 
The Otters And Rivers Project (OARP) is a national initiative supported by Water UK and 
The Wildlife Trusts.  At the local level in Cornwall, it is sponsored by Pennon Water 
Champions (South West Water and Viridor Waste Management), Cornwall Wildlife Trust and 
the Environment Agency. The project was successfully established in Cornwall in March 
1999. 
Key aims of the OARP are to restore the otter population, to increase knowledge of 
other semi-aquatic mammals and to advise on wetland habitat management, through a 
network of volunteers and partners.  Cornwall and Devon have the highest population of 
otters in England and in recognition of this the Camel Catchment in Cornwall is a candidate 
Special Area of Conservation primarily for otters.  It involves some 620 hectares of land and 
74 kilometres of river. 
 
 
2   THE OARP WORK REMIT 
 
The OARP has a wide ranging work remit including: 
 
2.1   Planning and policy 
The Project acts as a central contact point, collating and providing information for members 
of the public and others.  We support other projects on a local, regional and national scale, 
including Britain’s first ever Wildlife Veterinary Investigation Centre (VIC).  The OARP 
nominated Vic Simpson, for a national BBC Animal Award, for establishing the Wildlife VIC 
and his pioneering otter post mortem research.  We are delighted to say he won! 
 
2.2   Advisory work  
Roads pose one of the biggest threats and in four years over 100 corpses have been collected 
in the south west of England.  The OARP is working with the Highways Authority to install 
mitigation measures, such as ledges under bridges.  Otter predation at fisheries is increasing 
and can have a significant impact on stocks and revenue.  The OARP has also worked with 
the Royal Society for the Protection of Cruelty to Animals (RSPCA) and the wildlife police 
on a number of sinister otter deaths.  
 
2.3   Volunteers 
Volunteers are vital to the OARP.  Cornwall has a steadily increasing number of over 150 
Otter “Spotters” who are trained to look for otter signs and help monitor sites.  
 
 2.4  Surveying and monitoring 
The OARP surveyed 160 sites in Cornwall for the fourth National Otter Survey of England.  
The percentage of positive sites in the south west has increased from 24% (1977-79), 44% 
(1984-86), 67% (1991-94) to 83% (2000-02).  Other sites are also surveyed. 


2.5   Publicity and promotion 
The Project cannot acquire new “Otter Spotters” or give advice if no–one knows where we are 
or what we do.  We have held a range of talks, walks, workshops and conferences.  The 
OARP has achieved good media coverage, which includes national television and over 250 
newspaper and magazine articles. 
 
2.6  Species and habitats 
The water vole (Arvicola terrestris) is Britain’s fastest declining mammal.  Following several 
surveys by the OARP it is feared it could be extinct in Cornwall.  The American mink 
(Mustela vison) is being monitored to determine the need for its control.  The status of key 
wetland habitats, such as reedbeds, are also being assessed. 
 
 
3   THE FUTURE 
 
In 2002 the OARP was re-launched as Water for Wildlife (WfW).  WfW has a wider wetland 
remit, with the aim of protecting and enhancing our wetlands and their wildlife for the future.  
This includes identifying and surveying wetlands, providing advice to landowners and 
managers, carrying out a range of practical work including habitat restoration and creation 
and conducting monitoring programmes.  It also involves raising awareness of the state of our 
wetlands and their associated species - and what we can do to protect them.  
WfW embraces the aims of the biodiversity initiative.  But with 400 kilometres of 
Cornish coastline and 5000 kilometres plus of watercourse, not to mention other wetland 
habitats, there is a lot to be done.  WfW cannot do this alone and nor should it – we all have a 
responsibility to maintain biodiversity.  Thank you to all those supporting the OARP so far.  
We look forward to developing WfW and working with new partners.
